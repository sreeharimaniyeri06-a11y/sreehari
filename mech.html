What is Mechanical Engineering?

Mechanical engineering is one of the oldest and broadest engineering branches. It combines principles of physics, mathematics, and materials science to design everything from microscopic sensors to massive spacecraft. Key Pillars of Study

As a student or practitioner, you will naturally dive deep into these primary concepts:

  • Thermodynamics: Understanding heat, energy generation, and how power converts through engine frameworks.
  • Fluid Mechanics: Analyzing how liquids and gases behave under varying operational pressures.
  • Kinematics & Dynamics: Calculating physical motions, structural forces, and structural stress thresholds.
  • CAD Design (Computer-Aided Design): Building complex computer models and mechanical drawings before physical fabrication.
